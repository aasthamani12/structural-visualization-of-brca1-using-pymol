# Structural Visualization and Functional Interpretation of BRCA1 Using PyMOL

## Overview

This project focuses on the visualization and structural interpretation of the human BRCA1 protein using PyMOL. The AlphaFold-predicted structure of BRCA1 was retrieved and analyzed through multiple visualization techniques to better understand its three-dimensional architecture, secondary structural organization, molecular surface topology, and spatial arrangement.

The project demonstrates how molecular visualization tools can be applied in structural bioinformatics to explore protein structure and gain insights into protein organization.

---

## Objectives

* Visualize the three-dimensional structure of BRCA1.
* Analyze secondary structural elements including α-helices, β-sheets, and loop regions.
* Explore the molecular surface topology of the protein.
* Examine the protein from multiple orientations to understand its spatial organization.
* Gain practical experience in protein structural visualization using PyMOL.

---

## Materials and Tools

* PyMOL
* AlphaFold Protein Structure Database
* UniProt
* BRCA1 Protein (UniProt ID: P38398)

---

## Workflow

1. Retrieve the AlphaFold-predicted structure of BRCA1.
2. Download the structure file in PDB format.
3. Import the structure into PyMOL.
4. Generate an overall three-dimensional visualization.
5. Examine secondary structural elements.
6. Create cartoon representations of the protein.
7. Generate molecular surface visualizations.
8. Analyze alternative structural orientations.
9. Capture and document visual observations.
10. Interpret structural features and organization.

---

## Key Observations

* BRCA1 exhibits a complex three-dimensional architecture.
* The structure contains both ordered and flexible regions.
* Secondary structural elements are concentrated within compact regions.
* Surface visualization highlights the irregular and extended nature of the protein.
* Multiple structural orientations improve understanding of protein organization.

---

## Conclusion

This project demonstrates the application of PyMOL in protein structural visualization and interpretation. Through visualization of the AlphaFold-predicted BRCA1 structure, insights were obtained into protein architecture, secondary structure organization, and molecular topology. The project highlights the importance of computational visualization tools in structural bioinformatics.
